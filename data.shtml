Data access

Because VPHAS+ is an ESO Public Survey, all data will be released to the public. Initial public data products include reduced images and unstacked single-band source lists. In the future, we envisage that a well-validated catalogue will be made available that provides 5 optical photometric data points per source at an external (systematic) precision of 0.02—0.03 magnitudes on around 200 million objects.

Data release 1
The first public data release, VPHAS-DR1, was made available in 2013 through a dedicated page on the ESO website. The release covers the first 9 months of data-taking in which a tenth of the survey footprint was observed to desired quality. These first pointings have favoured the Galactic equator and some well-known open clusters of interest to the Gaia-ESO Survey. The contents of the release are described in an accompanying document and the reduced images and unstacked single-band source lists can be accessed through the ESO phase 3 query interface.
