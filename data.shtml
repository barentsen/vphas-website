Data access

Because VPHAS+ is an ESO Public Survey, all data will be released to the public. Initial public data products include reduced images and unstacked single-band source lists. In the future, we envisage that well-validated catalogues will be made available that provide 5 optical photometric data points per source at an external (systematic) precision of 0.02—0.03 magnitudes on, finally, over 300 million objects. From late 2014, work has started on the first of these.

Data release 1
The first public data release, VPHAS-DR1, was made available in 2013 through a dedicated page on the ESO website. The release covers the first 9 months of data-taking in which a tenth of the survey footprint was observed to desired quality. These first pointings have favoured the Galactic equator and some well-known open clusters of interest to the Gaia-ESO Survey. The contents of the release are described in an accompanying document and the reduced images and unstacked single-band source lists can be accessed through the ESO phase 3 query interface.

Data release 2
The second public data release, VPHAS-DR2, is being made available in early 2015 through ESO's dedicated web pages in the same way as VPHAS-DR1. The release covers the first 21 months of data-taking in which the survey progressed to 24 percent completion. The bias in favour of the Galactic equator continues to the extent that much of the mid-Plane is now done (especially in the r, i and Ha filters). The contents of the release are described in a second release document (current draft) and the reduced images and unstacked single-band source lists will again be accessible through the ESO phase 3 query interface, after data upload.

On this page we will be providing links to advice and software that will aid users in customised bandmerging of the released data.

Based on data products from observations made with ESO Telescopes at the La Silla Paranal Observatory under programme ID 177.D-3023, as part of the VST Photometric Hα Survey of the Southern Galactic Plane and Bulge (VPHAS+, www.vphas.eu).

Known issues
Like any other data set, VPHAS+ is affected by a number of caveats. Please refer to the dedicated web-page on known issues to learn more.
