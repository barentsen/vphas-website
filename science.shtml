Science using VPHAS+ data

VPHAS+ will reveal emission line objects of the following types:

  • Wolf-Rayet and other massive evolved emission line stars
  • compact planetary nebulae and rapidly evolving post-AGB stars
  • Be stars of all types - including young Herbig stars
  • clustered and field T Tau stars
  • a range of interacting binary stars: symbiotics, 'supersoft' compact binaries, WD/NS/BH accreting binaries

Different filter combinations support effective selection of the following:

  • white dwarfs, horizontal branch stars and large numbers of early A-type stars via strong Hα absorption
  • OB stars and other intrinsically blue objects
  • post common-envelope binaries
  • red giants and dwarfs, located within distinct colour-colour tracks

The Hα narrowband images reveal diffuse ionised nebulae of all types: HII regions, supernova remnants, spatially-resolved planetary nebulae. The sensitivity of the r-Hα colour to intrinsic stellar colour can also be exploited along with the broadband data to trace line-of-sight extinction, at high angular resolution.
